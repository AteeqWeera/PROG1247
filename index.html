Rumi (Jalauddin Balkhi): The Mystic Poet

This website is about Rumi, the 12th Century persian poet and his works.


Rumi was born in Balkh, Afghanistan in 1207. He was a persian poet, Islamic scholar,
theologian and Sufi mystic. He is best known for his poetry which has been translated into
many languages.
His works are widely read in Iran, Afghanistan, Tajikistan, Turkey and the United States.

biography

Rumi was born in Balkh, in present-day Afghanistan,
and later moved to Konya, in present-day Turkey, where he spent most of his life.

Poetry

"Rumi's poetry is characterized by themes of love, mysticism, and the search for divine truth."

I AM THINE AND THOU ART MINE Rumi (trans. by R.A. Nicholson)
Eternal Life is gained by utter abandonment of one’s own life.
When God appears to His ardent lover, the lover is absorbed in Him,
and not so much as a hair of the lover remains.
True lovers are as shadows, and when the sun shines in glory the shadows vanish away.
He is a true lover to God to whom God says “I am thine and thou art Mine.”

Rumi's poetry delves into themes of love, mysticism, and the human
connection to the divine. His works are still widely read and appreciated today.

Legacy

"Rumi's influence transcends cultural and religious boundaries, making him
one of the most beloved poets in history."

Rumi's works have had a profound impact on literature and spirituality,
with his influence reaching far beyond his own time and place.

Famous Quote

One of Rumi's famous quotes: "The minute I heard my first love story, I started looking for you,
not knowing how blind that was. Lovers don't finally meet somewhere. They're in each other all along."
― Mawlana Jalal-al-Din Rumi, The Illuminated Rumi

Family Life

Rumi was married twice and had several children, including his son Sultan Walad,
who played a significant role in preserving and promoting Rumi's works.
